The Art of Our Hands

Every Ronak's Couture piece is a convergence of India's finest heritage textiles and master-level embellishment — materials and techniques that cannot be authentically replicated.

Heritage Textiles

Patola

Double-ikat silk woven in Patan, Gujarat — one of the rarest textile arts in the world. Each saree can take six months to complete on the loom.

Banarasi

Handwoven brocade from the looms of Varanasi, featuring gold and silver zari threads. The fabric of royal Indian weddings for centuries.

Bandhani

The ancient Gujarati art of tie-dye, where thousands of tiny knots are hand-tied to create intricate patterns before dyeing — each dot placed with precision.

Ikat & Kota

Resist-dyed weaves from Rajasthan and Gujarat. Lightweight yet rich in texture — ideal for suits and flowing silhouettes that carry the warmth of handcraft.

Master Workmanship

Zardozi

Mughal-era embroidery using gold and silver metallic threads on silk. Our artisans create raised, sculptural motifs that catch light like jewels on fabric.

Aari Work

Chain-stitch embroidery using a hooked needle, producing intricate patterns of extraordinary fineness. The foundation of our signature detailing on suits and lehengas.

Mirror Work

Hand-stitched reflective embellishments rooted in Kutch, Gujarat. Each mirror is individually set and secured — adding luminous movement to every silhouette.

Mochi Work

A centuries-old chain-stitch embroidery born from Gujarat's Mochi (cobbler) community in Kutch, using a hooked aari needle to create dense, luminous silk motifs once commissioned by royalty.

Pure Silk Foundations

Every garment begins with the finest base — pure silk, pure cotton, pure heritage. We never compromise on the foundation that holds our craft together.
